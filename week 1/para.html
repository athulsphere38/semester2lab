The tiger is one of the most magnificent and powerful creatures in the animal kingdom. Known for its striking orange coat with bold black stripes, each tiger has a unique stripe pattern, much like a human fingerprint. Tigers are carnivorous mammals and belong to the cat family, Felidae. They are skilled hunters, relying on stealth, strength, and sharp senses to catch their prey, which often includes deer, wild boar, and other animals. Found mainly in forests, grasslands, and mangrove swamps, tigers play a crucial,
role in maintaining the balance of their ecosystems. Sadly, they are an endangered species due to habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict. Conservation efforts, such as wildlife sanctuaries and anti-poaching laws, are vital to protect these majestic animals for future generations.
